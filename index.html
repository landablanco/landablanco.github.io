About

I study how psychological, relational, and structural factors shape mental health and interpersonal violence in low- and middle-income contexts, with sustained empirical attention to the Honduran population.

My work spans intimate partner violence, adverse and benevolent childhood experiences, suicide risk, gender attitudes, and the personality correlates of well-being. Methodologically, it relies on psychometric validation, regression and mediation modelling, latent class and factor analytic approaches, and qualitative designs including interviews, focus groups, and thematic coding.

Beyond my own program of research, I coordinate research and ethics functions within UNAH's School of Psychological Sciences and contribute to large cross-cultural consortia spanning more than 100 countries, examining mate preferences, social norms, and global health attitudes.

I am also a practicing clinical psychologist trained in CBT and ACT, which keeps my research grounded in applied questions about help-seeking, stigma, and intervention.

Research Focus

Ψ

Interpersonal & Gender-Based Violence

Predictors of IPV perpetration and victimization, corporal punishment attitudes, violence desensitization, and barriers to help-seeking — including the under-studied case of male victims.

Ψ

Mental Health & Help-Seeking

Depression, anxiety, suicide risk, and the psychological burden of the COVID-19 pandemic, alongside attitudes toward professional psychological help in university and general populations.

Ψ

Childhood Experiences

Adverse and benevolent childhood experiences as antecedents of adult flourishing, emotion regulation, risk behavior, and problematic technology use.

Ψ

Personality & Flourishing

Light and Dark Triad traits, narcissism, and positive psychology constructs as mediators of well-being, academic engagement, and digital behavior.

Ψ

Psychometrics & Measurement

Validation and cross-cultural adaptation of screening instruments — flourishing, depression, anxiety, somatization, and novel scales for violence desensitization.

Ψ

Cross-Cultural Collaboration

Contributing Honduran data to global consortia on romantic love, mate preferences, social norms, and health attitudes across 90–175 countries.
